Making new connections in Central Europe

Forming part of one of the world’s longest railway tunnels, the Semmering base tunnel is one of the most important major infrastructure projects in Central Europe. 

Key information

Location:
Austria
Date started:
1
Completion date:
Ongoing
Client type:
Infrastructure
Main services:
Cost and commercial management
  • 27.3km tunnel
  • 230km/hour train speed

The Semmering base tunnel’s two-tube railway tunnel will supplement and ease the pressure on the current mountain line from Gloggnitz to Mürzzuschlag, which forms part of the Ghega railway track, a UNESCO World Cultural Heritage site.

It will consist of two parallel guided tunnels with a diameter of approx 10m and a gap of 40-70m between one another. They are to be intermittently connected to each another at a maximum distance of 500m by accessible cross passages, i.e. cross-cuts.

  • Vehicle speed 230km/h
  • Freight trains up to 1,600t
  • 27.3km tunnel with NATM (new Austrian tunnelling method) and TBM driving systems
  • Five shafts with up to 450m depth
  • Emergency stop underground
  • Landfill up to 425 million m³

Our contribution

We are responsible for the project supervision of three tunnel sections (SBT1.1, SBT2.1 and SBT3.1) in respect to the cost efficiency and effectiveness for the implementation phase of the aforementioned projects.
 
Turner & Townsend also advises the ÖBB-Infrastruktur AG in both technical and financial respects. The fields of activity include project organisation, scheduling and cost tracking, quotation as well as additional costs audits, risk management and the cost reduction programmes. We provide key advice right from the start so that the client can implement our recommendations immediately to reduce and/or prevent possible risks and additional costs.

Making the difference

We will bring extensive knowledge and expertise to the work assignments encompassed within the Semmering base tunnel, supporting ÖBB-Infrastruktur AG in the professional implementation of the project with our best practice methods. Using a modern online reporting system allows us access to all data relevant to the project and facilitates easy analysis and insight-sharing.
