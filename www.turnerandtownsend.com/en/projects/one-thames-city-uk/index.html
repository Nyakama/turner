One Thames City, UK

Explore
  1. Home
  2. Projects
  3. One Thames City, UK

Supporting a high-end residential scheme in the UK capital.

Delivering almost 1,500 new homes, shops, restaurants, and commercial space centered around 2.5 acres of public open space, the One Thames City development is one of Chinese developer R&F Properties’ first major projects in London.

Key information

Location:
United Kingdom
Date started:
2018
Completion date:
2023
Client type:
Real estate
Main services:
Project management and contract administration, cost and commercial management, procurement, programme management, programme strategy and set-up

High-rise residential

R&F Properties appointed us as its employer’s representative for project management and cost management services as it set out to deliver this mixed-use scheme in south west London’s Nine Elms area.

The premium residences will be surrounded by facilities in the heart of the UK capital and at 52 storeys, one of the 12 buildings will be the tallest residential building in London’s zone one, completing late next year.

Adding value

With our in-depth knowledge of the London market, we added value to the client’s delivery of this exciting project by leading on the procurement, contract administration, programme management, cost management, establishment of project processes and governance and the general day-to-day management of their project.

Sustainable outlook

One Thames City is aiming to be highly sustainable: it connects into Wandsworth Borough Council’s district heating network that serves a number of sites in the wider Nine Elms development area. The project has sought to procure, where possible, sustainable, ethical and responsibly sourced and certified materials, supporting the BREEAM targets with the design currently achieving a 23 percent carbon reduction.

What is more, the scheme is being designed to take into account changing living and working habits. The layouts of the residential towers have been designed to allow for flexible use by incorporating home-office study to accommodate flexible ways of working, while the commercial elements of the development are being designed to reflect emerging trends in the workplace and provide a flexible workplace.
