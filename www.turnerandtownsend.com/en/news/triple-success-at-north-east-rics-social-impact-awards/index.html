Triple success at North East RICS Social Impact Awards

04 May 2020

Our Newcastle team has enjoyed triple success at the North East RICS Social Impact Awards, which recognise the built environment's positive and transformational contribution to society.

Life Kitchen at Mowbray Park Lodge, Sunderland was named best project in the Leisure category and overall project of the year, Biosphere at the Helix in Newcastle was also named best project in the Commercial category.

Life Kitchen runs free cookery classes for people living with cancer where treatments can frequently inhibit taste. In June 2019, it opened its first permanent cookery school at the Grade II listed Mowbray Park Lodge. It has since received grant funding to provide over 600 classes to the Clinical Commissioning Group Sunderland, Northern Cancer Alliance and World Cancer Research Fund.

Project Director Paul Humble led the project and was instrumental in securing pro-bono support to create the Life Kitchen facility. As a result, all construction-related services, labour, plant and materials were provided free-of-charge from over 40 organisations based in the region.

Located at Helix, Newcastle’s 24 acre mixed-use city-centre development, the Biosphere provides 7,200m2 of laboratory and office space. In addition to raising the profile of the city for biosciences and inspiring the next generation of science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) workers, the facility is projected to add £160m gross value to the regional economy. We provided cost management services for the project pre-contract.

Darren Laybourn, director and strategic lead, Newcastle and Teesside, said:

These two projects have already made a huge social impact on the north east, the result of a huge collaborative effort with our project partners.

"Life Kitchen has brought out the absolute best in the region’s construction industry to help provide an amazing service to cancer patients, while the Biosphere further reinforces Newcastle’s credentials as the UK hub for biosciences. I am immensely proud of our team in the north east and they deserve every credit for their part in these projects.”

Ryan Riley, co-founder of Life Kitchen, said:

I am absolutely thrilled to win the leisure category and regional project of the year. We are so appreciative of all the efforts the team made on the build. This prestigious award is as much for them as it is for Life Kitchen.

See the full list of winners at the RICS Social Impact Awards.
