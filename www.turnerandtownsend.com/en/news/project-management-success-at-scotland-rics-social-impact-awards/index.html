Project management success at Scotland RICS Social Impact Awards

23 April 2020

Our team in Scotland project managed two winning projects at the Scotland RICS Social Impact Awards, which recognise the built environment's positive and transformational contribution to society. The Prince and Princess of Wales Hospice was named best project in the Healthcare category and The Event Complex Aberdeen (TECA) was named best project in the Commercial category.

The Prince and Princess of Wales Hospice is a new facility in Glasgow, providing an exceptional standard of end of life care for over 1,200 patients and families annually.

Based on a Scandinavian model of care and designed by Ryder Architecture, the new energy-efficient facility enables the hospice to extend its care to young adults of +16 years.

As well as providing project management services we were also involved in supporting the fundraising efforts of this self-funded project. 

TECA is a 105,000sqm flexible facility, capable of hosting world-class exhibitions as well as a wide variety of entertainment events.

We provided project management and PMO services through the eight years of its development and were instrumental for Henry Boot Developments in their appointment with Aberdeen City Council in the initial competitive dialogue stage.

Andy Outram, Director, said:

The Prince and Princess of Wales Hospice and The Event Complex Aberdeen are two very special projects. I am delighted that their human, social and environmental impact has been recognised and proud that our team has played a project management role in their delivery.

“It is particularly pleasing that the Prince and Princess of Wales Hospice has received their honour. The project was conceived over 15 years ago, and through the exemplary fundraising efforts of their CEO and their staff, together with the generosity of the people of Glasgow, they raised £23m to build an incredible new home.”
