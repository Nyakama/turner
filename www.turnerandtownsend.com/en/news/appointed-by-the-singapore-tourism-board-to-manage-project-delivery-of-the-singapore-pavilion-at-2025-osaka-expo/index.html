Appointed by the Singapore Tourism Board to manage project delivery of the Singapore Pavilion at 2025 Osaka Expo

27 March 2023

We have been appointed by Singapore Tourism Board (STB) to provide consultancy services to manage the project delivery of the Singapore Pavilion at World Expo 2025 to be held in Osaka, Japan from 13 April to 13 October 2025.

We will provide strategic project-management and quantity-surveying services to STB during key delivery stages of the Singapore Pavilion, comprising turnkey vendor evaluation, construction, internal fit-out, exhibit installations, pavilion operations and maintenance, demolition, reinstatement, and project account settlement.

Over 120 countries have confirmed participation at the 2025 Osaka Expo, with an estimated 28 million visitors expected over the six-month duration according to the Japan Association for the 2025 World Exposition. The theme for the event is ‘Designing Future Society for Our Lives’ as it aims to be a key platform to ramp up efforts to achieve the United Nation’s Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) by 2030.

We have previously supported the development and successful delivery of six country pavilions at the World Expo 2020 in Dubai, including the Netherlands Pavilion, which received industry acclaim with awards including ‘Best Sustainable Construction Project of the Year’ at The Big 5 Construction Impact Awards, and ‘Best Architecture & Landscape Award’ at the BIE Official Expo Participant Award.

Khoo Sze Boon, Managing Director, Singapore and Vietnam comments:

It is an honour to work with Singapore Tourism Board to showcase the best of the country to the world at World Expo 2025 in Osaka.

"It is also a huge opportunity for our cross-border team to build on our success of delivering over 50 projects in the past year across Japan and Singapore, and to leverage our international expertise from the successful World Expo 2020 in Dubai.”

“Singapore Tourism Board recognised our comprehensive approach to project management and quantity surveying, and we are looking forward to working closely with them to showcase the innovative spirit of Singapore’s homegrown enterprises and the nation’s story to the world.”

Image provided by: Japan Association for the 2025 World Exposition.
