Vice President, New York

North America

Economic overview: Recession fears remain

Speculation regarding an imminent recession remains prevalent as the Federal Reserve Board (Fed) continues to increase interest rates in response to the high general inflation rate. In addition, preparations are being taken by firms to limit the impact of the potential recession on the horizon. Despite ongoing uncertainty, the US economy stood steadfast during Q4 2022, with increases recorded in year-over-year gross domestic product and personal consumption expenditure.

From September 2022 to January 2023, the unemployment rate has remained stable in the mid three-percent range, which is indicative of a robust and healthy labour market.

Recent mass lay-offs in the tech sector have sparked economic concerns but may not have been an indicator of a forthcoming recession so much as a correction of inflated employment levels during the pandemic. Although the layoffs have left thousands of tech employees without jobs, mass reductions in force have not been reciprocated in other sectors.

Construction market remains resilient

Economic uncertainty is one of several challenges that face the construction industry at the end of Q1 2023. Sustained supply-chain issues continue to exert pressures on pricing and availability of materials, causing extreme volatility of material pricing depending on region.

The construction market, however, has proven resilient against recessionary pressures, barring the shrinking 2023 residential market. As of March 2023, year-over-year escalation continues to ease over 2022 peaks. Many cities are approaching the 2.7 percent escalation average seen in 2016 through to 2020. Although supply and labour challenges remain, lower escalation rates suggest these challenges are easing.
