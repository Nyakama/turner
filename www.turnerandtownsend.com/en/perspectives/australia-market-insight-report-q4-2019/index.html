Australia market insight report Q4 2019

Simon Kearney

Director - Real estate

Australia and New Zealand

Email +61 282 450 000
Simon Kearney 11 March 2020

With global population heading towards the eight billion people mark, pressure on available space is only set to get more acute.

Our Q4 2019 Australian market insight report highlights that the construction sector continues to perform strongly.

Cost escalation in construction is reported as 4.5 percent in Sydney, 4 percent in Melbourne, 2.5 percent in Brisbane and 1 percent in Perth.

The current capacity of the supply chain varies significantly between states which generally reflects the current stage of the industry life cycle. New South Wales and Victoria’s supply chains are above capacity while Queensland and Western Australia are borderline between slow and normal.

Over station development: doing more with less

The national infrastructure spend is continuing to absorb material and labour resources, and in Sydney in particular, the construction of central business district (CBD) metro stations is having an impact on commercial vacancy rates driving demand for new space to be constructed.

In ever-growing and crowded cities, over station development could make better use of the space above our cities, stimulate economic growth, and reshape how urban areas will be organised and function for generations to come.
