Understanding complexity in life sciences

The life sciences sector is at the forefront of innovation, spanning pharmaceuticals, biomedical technologies, diagnostics and digital health, animal health, agribusiness and more. Like the global population it serves, the sector is growing,

To meet the challenges of globalisation, consumer demands and fierce competition, the sector is retrofitting, expanding and building new facilities. Projects and programmes can be highly complex, with pressurised timescales and costs to deliver on business plan ambitions fast within this intense commercial environment.

Scientific research campuses, buildings, laboratories and production facilities have to comply with exacting technical, safety and regulatory requirements. These demanding large-scale projects may include a range of building types, from offices to energy centres, and require specialised utility services and zones, such as high containment areas and cleanrooms.
