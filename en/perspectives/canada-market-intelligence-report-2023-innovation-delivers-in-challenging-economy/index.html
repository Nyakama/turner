Our 2023 annual report for Canada provides market and regional analyses, sector insights, and the latest cost guide for hard construction in major markets. Despite a tough economic market, innovative technology, net-zero targets and project controls are delivering successful outcomes.

Economic overview: growth loses momentum Â 

A series of new challenges have confronted the Canadian economy over the past year. Supply chain disruptions, high rates of inflation, a tight labour market and rapidly increasing interest rates have played a role in testing business confidence. As the Bank of Canada maintains its fight against inflation is a top priority, we expect interest rates to play a major role in influencing market conditions in 2023

Despite the prospect of pushing the economy into a recession, curbing inflation remains the top priority for the Bank of Canada, a position many central banks are currently facing.

Elevated rates of business uncertainty is reflected in a business confidence index, which experienced a fall from 51.4 to just 33.4 from November to December 2022. Â 

We have also seen recent signs of cooling consumer spending. In addition, layoffs at several major employers may indicate the start of a softening job market and a further challenge to consumer resilience in Canada.
