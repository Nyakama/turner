Backing the UK2070 Commission to tackle regional inequalities

27 February 2020

We have been privileged to continue our relationship with the UK2070 Commission as active supporters, and contributors to its work.

The UK2070 Commission’s final report, launched today at Media City in Manchester, says that drastic action is needed to address spatial inequalities – from jobs and housing, to climate – that have built up over generations to leave the UK one of the most unequal large countries in the developed world.

We have continued to support and contribute to the UK2070 programme. Through the provision of a Commissioner we have brought to bear our UK-wide expertise in major programmes across the infrastructure, real estate and natural resources sectors. 

The Commission’s final report sets out a clear ‘Programme of Action’ to address inequality in the UK, ranging from ensuring that the transition to a zero-carbon economy balances the needs of all communities, through to backing modern industry, delivering a connectivity revolution and the establishment of new centres of excellence.

Patricia Moore, Managing Director UK, said that the Commission’s work provided opportunities for the construction sector, but also an obligation for the industry to focus on better outcomes for communities through its work:

The UK2070 Commission’s second report shows that regional inequalities across the UK are no longer tolerable. Regional development sorely needs a stimulus to provide an integrated approach giving us continuity and a long-term plan.

"We welcome the ten point programme for action put forward by the Commission. There are particular issues within this programme that we, within the construction industry, must play an active part in addressing. We cannot, and indeed must not, keep doing things the way we are today. 

"Our industry will not succeed in creating change if we continue with fragmented, underpowered and short-term initiatives. We need a major cultural shift. This requires us to collaborate and move to a more outcomes-based mindset.”

The ten point programme from the UK2070 Commission’s report includes:

  1. A spatially just transition to zero carbon: Ensuring there is an explicit spatial dimension in the UK’s plan to become zero carbon by 2050.
  2. Delivering a connectivity revolution: Creating a transformed public transport network between cities, within cities and beyond cities.
  3. Creating new global centres of excellence: Harnessing increased investment in research and development to create ‘hub and spoke’ networks of excellence across the country to complement London and the Wider South East.
  4. Strengthening the foundations of local economies: Empowering local leadership in towns and local communities to deliver increased economic growth and well-being.
  5. Rethinking the housing crisis: Recognising housing as part of national infrastructure and ensuring that supply of new housing meets the needs of the economy.
  6. Harnessing cultural and environmental assets: Increasing the focus of policy and funding of assets outside of London.
  7. Implementing a comprehensive framework for inclusive devolution: Allow different places to step up through different levels of devolution according to local ambition, need and capacity.
  8. Future skilling the United Kingdom: Develop a national plan to raise attainment levels, especially in future skill needs for all areas to achieve the levels of the best performing places.
  9. Levelling up the playing field: fairer access to funds: Triple the size of the Shared Prosperity Fund to £15bn per annum for 20 years with clear spatial priorities; and change the way major projects and local priorities are able to be funded and assessed.
  10. Shaping the future: A National Spatial Plan for England Task the National Infrastructure Commission to create a national spatial plan for England and linking to those in Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland, to guide investment and to support local and regional spatial plans.

View the UK2070 Commission’s final report for full details.
