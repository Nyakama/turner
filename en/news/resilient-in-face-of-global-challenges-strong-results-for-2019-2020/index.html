Resilient in face of global challenges, strong results for 2019-2020

21 September 2020

We have reported turnover of £744m for the year ending 30 April 2020, despite the impact of COVID-19 on the last quarter. Our resilience and success continue to be driven by investing in talent, shifting focus to emerging markets, addressing the world’s biggest challenges and building solutions that drive exceptional client outcomes.

Working on some of the world’s largest and most complex capital projects and programmes across real estate, infrastructure and natural resources, we increased net revenue by £101m, to £681m, a 17 percent increase on 2018-19. In the UK, net revenue rose by nine percent in 2019-20, to £283m.

Data-driven real estate

We delivered strong growth in our real estate business, with net revenue climbing 19 percent to £404m, driven by demand from the high-tech and life sciences sectors.

During the last financial year, we acquired Taurus Project Controls Consulting in Boston and invested in expanding our portfolio management capability across regional and global programmes, including the launch of new technology platform pace> to enable clients to monitor progress and data on their real estate portfolios in real-time.

Driving performance on complex infrastructure programmes

Our infrastructure net revenue rose 15 percent to £206m, with robust growth in the UK, North America, the Middle East, Asia and Australia. Continuing our success in rail, we are working on a range of major programmes including Sydney Inland Rail and New Zealand’s largest infrastructure project, the underground City Rail.

Supporting net zero transition

Despite a turbulent market and collapsing global energy prices, our natural resources revenue hit £71m, up 16 percent on 2018-19. With a focus on supporting net zero transition, we have successfully built market share in renewables, working across a number of liquefied natural gas (LNG) projects, including Freeport – the second-largest LNG facility in North America.

We also invested in a programme management proposition for mine closure and rehabilitation, and are currently working for De Beers to rehabilitate former mining sites worldwide to support their future land use.

We have committed to reducing our own environmental impact – setting and meeting internal targets for carbon reduction and ensuring that sustainability and social value principles are actively embedded in our working culture.

Targets set in 2015 to cut carbon emissions per employee by 25 percent have already been met.

Our Chairman and CEO Vincent Clancy said:

“While we, like the rest of the industry, continue to feel the impact of COVID-19, our financial performance and business model stand us in good stead to withstand the economic aftershocks.

Resilience has been the hallmark of our business over the last 74 years and this, combined with the solid platform we have created, makes me confident that we will emerge even stronger.

“As we transition from a health crisis to meet a growing economic challenge, the world needs to embrace a zero-carbon future, to transform cities and to create better living standards.

Our industry is fundamental to solving the social and economic inequalities highlighted by the COVID-19 crisis, but in the past has too often been part of the problem. "We now have an exciting opportunity to drive change and to build a better built environment.”
