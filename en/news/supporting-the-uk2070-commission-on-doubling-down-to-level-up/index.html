Supporting the UK2070 Commission on Doubling Down to Level Up

01 October 2020

We remain active supporters of the UK2070 Commission and continue to contribute to its work to address the UK’s regional inequalities.

The UK2070 Commission’s new report, Go Big. Go Local, has been launched today, setting out the implications of the COVID-19 pandemic for the government’s Levelling Up agenda.

The latest report welcomes the Prime Minister’s commitment to ‘doubling-down’ on Levelling Up in the UK, whilst at the same time highlighting the risk of a deep economic recession resulting from the pandemic that could further exacerbate existing economic and social divisions between our cities, regions and nations.

The report also follows the ten point plan published by the Commission in February 2020, before the pandemic struck. This identified the priorities for economic growth, infrastructure renewal and institutional reform to overcome the deep-rooted regional inequalities.

Building on these recommendations, the Commission now urges action at scale. Its latest report details a clear plan towards recovery, the rebalancing required and the milestones and outcomes needed for success.

Patricia Moore, Managing Director UK, commented on the Commission’s latest report: “The UK2070 Commission has been consistently raising the alarm about the UK’s deep-rooted inequalities since before the pandemic struck. As we look to stimulate the economy, we must do this in the context of rebalancing the long standing social and environmental divisions up and down the country.

The key enabler for the UK’s future growth is infrastructure, but our industry needs to step up to the plate if we are to make the Levelling Up agenda a reality.

"This means modernising the way we work and increasing productivity, providing cost-certainty and assuring the benefits of major projects and programmes with sustainable solutions.

If we can get this right, not only will we rebalance social and economic equalities close to home, but we will also build exportable expertise that celebrates the work in our industry on a world stage.”

We support the UK2070 Commission’s programme and have contributed to its latest report bringing to bear our UK-wide expertise in major programmes across the infrastructure, real estate and natural resources sectors.

View the Commission’s report for full details.
