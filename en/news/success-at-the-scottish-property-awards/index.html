Success at the Scottish Property Awards

25 February 2020

We project managed two of the winning projects at the recent Scottish Property Awards. The Event Complex Aberdeen (TECA) won the Development of the Year (Commercial property), for our client HBD (Henry Boot Developments).

The 105,000sqm complex provides a flexible facility, capable of hosting world class exhibitions as well as a wide variety of entertainment events. We provided project management and PMO services through the eight years of its development, and were instrumental for HBD in their appointment with Aberdeen City Council in the initial competitive dialogue stage.

The Prince and Princess of Wales Hospice took the prizes for Innovation in Design and Healthcare Development of the Year. The new facility in Glasgow offers quality of life for patients in a place that looks and feels like home. The hospice is based on a Scandinavian model of care and was designed by Ryder Architecture. We provided project management and were also involved in supporting the funding raising efforts of this self-funded project.

Andy Outram, Director and Real Estate Leader for Scotland, said:

We are delighted to see the recognition for these two projects in Scotland and to have been leading the teams ensuring their successful delivery. We are extremely proud to have project managed both the phenomenal event centre for the North East of Scotland, and the unique and special hospice facility in Glasgow.
